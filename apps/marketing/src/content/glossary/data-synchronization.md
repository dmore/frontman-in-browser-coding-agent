This concept encompasses the continual updating and harmonization of information between sources, whether that means aligning records between databases, keeping files consistent across cloud storage platforms, or maintaining up-to-date application states in distributed environments. Synchronizing data is not limited to merely copying or transferring data; it involves complex mechanisms to track changes, resolve conflicts, and guarantee that updates are propagated accurately and promptly. As organizations increasingly adopt multi-cloud architectures and distributed systems, the demand for robust data management strategies to prevent discrepancies and data silos has intensified.
