<h2>What is Monorepo?</h2> <p>A Monorepo is a development strategy where multiple projects or components coexist within a single version-controlled repository. This approach organizes codebases for libraries, applications, and tools under one repository, streamlining collaboration and dependency management. Monorepos have become an influential concept in software engineering, particularly in large-scale environments where managing interdependent codebases effectively is crucial. They offer teams a unified place for versioning, facilitating atomic commits and consistent dependency updates. The practice enables teams to coordinate changes across projects and mitigate integration issues before deployment. By centralizing code management, Monorepos can enhance transparency and reduce the friction associated with multi-repository setups. According to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monorepo" target="_blank">Wikipedia’s overview</a>, the concept has roots dating back to the early 2000s, and it continues to be widely discussed in the engineering community. For related concepts such as <a href="https://frontman.sh/glossary/version-control" >version control</a>, Monorepos represent a strategic evolution, making collaborative development more streamlined and standardized.</p> <h2>Synonyms</h2> <ul><li>Monolithic Repository</li><li>Unified Codebase</li><li>Single Repository</li><li>Centralized Repository</li><li>Shared Codebase</li></ul> <h2>Examples of Monorepo Usage</h2> <p>Generalized scenarios illustrate the practical value of a Monorepo structure.
