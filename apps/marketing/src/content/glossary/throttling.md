<h2>What is Throttling?</h2><p>Throttling, in the context of technology and data management, refers to the intentional regulation of resource usage, typically involving a reduction or limitation of performance or access rates to maintain system stability. This mechanism is widely employed to control the flow of requests, data, or computational tasks, ensuring that servers, applications, or networks operate within their capacity and avoid overload. By modulating the speed or frequency of operations, throttling prevents resource exhaustion and minimizes the risk of downtime or degraded service quality. Its usage is prevalent across a spectrum of domains, from APIs and network traffic to hardware components such as CPUs. As systems scale and user demands fluctuate, the importance of implementing effective throttling strategies cannot be overstated. According to <a href="https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/throttling" target="_blank">Merriam-Webster</a>, the term has evolved from its original meaning—constriction or suppression—to signify controlled moderation in technical systems.
