Unlike standard hooks, which are provided by libraries such as React, these developer-defined functions allow for the organization and extraction of component logic into reusable units. This separation of concerns not only streamlines codebases but also enhances maintainability by reducing duplication. By leveraging such mechanisms, teams can standardize how data is fetched, managed, or manipulated. The concept aligns with the broader trend toward composability in software engineering, where modular, self-contained units of logic can be recombined to build complex interfaces.
