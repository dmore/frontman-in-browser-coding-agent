<h2>What is Accessible Name?</h2><p>The concept of an accessible name plays a foundational role in web accessibility and user interface design. It refers to the textual label or identifier given to user interface elements—such as buttons, form fields, links, and images—so that assistive technologies, including screen readers, can convey their purpose to users who may not interact visually with the content. By associating a concise and meaningful label, the accessible name ensures that every user, regardless of ability, receives essential context about how to navigate and interact with digital interfaces. The accurate assignment of these labels is vital for compliance with standards like the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), and for fostering an inclusive user experience. As noted in <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/ARIA/apg/practices/names-and-descriptions/" target="_blank">guidance from the W3C</a>, accessible names are typically brief but must provide clarity. The underlying principle is that digital components should never be ambiguous to assistive tools. Granular understanding of accessible names is critical for teams building robust, scalable, and universally usable products.
