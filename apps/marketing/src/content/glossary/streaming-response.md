<h2>What is Streaming Response?</h2><p>Streaming Response is a data delivery technique used in web and cloud computing to transmit information incrementally as it becomes available, rather than waiting for a complete response before sending data to the client. This approach leverages protocols and APIs that enable servers to push data in small chunks, allowing consumers to begin processing or rendering content immediately. By breaking down large payloads into manageable segments, Streaming Response reduces latency and enhances the perceived performance of applications. The technology is especially relevant for scenarios involving real-time data, such as live updates, conversational AI, or progressive data feeds. It forms a critical component in modern architectures where responsiveness and user experience are prioritized. Its adoption is closely tied to the evolution of web APIs and cloud platforms, with many frameworks incorporating support for stream-based delivery natively.
