<h2>What is Cross-Browser Compatibility</h2><p>Cross-browser compatibility refers to the ability of a website or web application to operate and display consistently across different web browsers, rendering engines, and device configurations. As browser vendors interpret HTML, CSS, and JavaScript specifications with subtle variations, developers face the ongoing challenge of ensuring that content appears and behaves as intended, regardless of the user's choice of browser or device. Without careful attention to compatibility, users may encounter broken layouts, missing features, or degraded performance. Addressing these variations can involve comprehensive testing, the use of polyfills, and adherence to web standards. The significance of robust compatibility has grown in parallel with the proliferation of devices and browsers, with the expectation that digital experiences remain seamless for all users.
