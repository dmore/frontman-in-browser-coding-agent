<h2>What is Structured Data?</h2><p>Structured data refers to information that adheres to a predefined model, making it easily searchable and accessible by both humans and machines. This information is typically organized into rows and columns, leveraging relational databases or spreadsheets for optimized storage and retrieval. The underlying schema or format ensures that each data element is placed within a specific context, allowing for efficient categorization and analysis. Structured data contrasts sharply with unstructured or semi-structured formats, as it relies on consistency and clear relationships between data points. Its prevalence across sectors has made it a cornerstone in business intelligence, analytics, and automation workflows. As organizations increasingly rely on data-driven decisions, structured information remains key to effective processing, supporting everything from transactional operations to reporting. Advances in data modeling and management have further cemented its importance, enabling interoperability across diverse systems and platforms.
